# Clarity

> Source: Apple Human Interface Guidelines — Clarity; Nielsen Norman Group Heuristic #8 (Aesthetic and Minimalist Design)

Clarity means that text is legible at every size, icons are precise and lucid, adornments are subtle and appropriate, and a sharpened focus on functionality motivates the design. Every element serves a purpose.

## Do examples

1. **Use sufficient contrast ratios.** Body text achieves WCAG AA (4.5:1) or better. This ensures readability across ambient lighting conditions and display types. Rationale: accessibility is not optional; low contrast causes measurable drop in task completion rates.

2. **Employ a clear visual hierarchy.** Headlines are visually distinct from body text through weight, size, and spacing — not color alone. Rationale: users scan before reading; hierarchy guides the eye to primary content within 300ms.

3. **Use precise, functional icons.** Each icon maps to exactly one action. Decorative icons are omitted unless they reinforce meaning (e.g., a lock icon next to "Secure checkout"). Rationale: ambiguous iconography increases error rates by 18% per NN/g eye-tracking studies.

## Don't examples

1. **Don't use thin, low-contrast text on textured backgrounds.** Light grey text on a slightly darker grey background fails contrast requirements and frustrates users with low vision. Rationale: violates WCAG and Apple HIG clarity guidance simultaneously.

2. **Don't overload screens with competing visual elements.** When every element demands attention (bold type, bright colors, animations), nothing stands out. Rationale: cognitive overload degrades decision-making speed.

3. **Don't use icons without labels for unfamiliar actions.** A hamburger menu is learned; a custom "magic wand" icon for "auto-enhance" is not. Rationale: recognition over recall is a foundational usability heuristic.

## Edge case: Clarity vs. Deference

When clarity demands a prominent label but deference suggests the chrome should recede, clarity wins for primary actions and deference wins for secondary actions. Resolution: primary CTAs get explicit labels; secondary actions use icon-only with tooltip on hover.
